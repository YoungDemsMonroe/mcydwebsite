Activist Toolkit

There is no one right way to be engaged. We have created a resource that is designed to be a hub that connects people with whatever form of engagement speaks to them. This document is not a policy briefing and won’t recommend a certain angle of engagement over another. Our vision is for a community in which young people are actively engaged in the issues that matter to them and have the knowledge and skillset to be able to enact change through their preferred mode.

Monroe County is uniquely positioned to be a driver of activism and involvement for progressives in the state of Indiana. Monroe County is home to Indiana University Bloomington, one of the top public research universities in the world, as well as a campus of Ivy Tech Community College, Indiana’s postsecondary institution. On average, residents of Monroe County are more educated, have more access to broadband internet, and have a working population (18-65) that is 10% higher than the average for the state of Indiana. For these reasons, the Monroe County Young Democrats have created this resource in order to mobilize young people to get involved on important issues and elections.
